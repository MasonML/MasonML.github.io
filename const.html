The Constitution of Mason Machine Learning


Article I - Name of Organization

The name of this organization shall be Mason Machine Learning (MML). The website for MML shall be located at masonml.github.io.

Article II - Purpose of Organization

MML aims to provide a forum for collaborative learning and software development in machine learning. Our goal is to significantly enrich the experiences of students through our work on Artificial Intelligence.

Article III - Membership

Membership in this organization will not be restricted on the basis of race, color, religion, ethnicity, national origin, physical or mental disability, sexual orientation, veteran status, gender identity, species, gender expression, sex, or age.

Membership is open to all currently enrolled GMU students in good academic standing with the university with a minimum cumulative GPA of at least 2.0 who support the advancement of the organization’s principles.

MML attendees will be classified as a member, developer, or researcher. In this document, the term “general membership” shall refer to the combined body of members, developers, and researchers. The term “esteemed membership” shall refer to the combined body of developers and researchers.

Members

Members are individuals interested in participating in the MML community. All new attendees of MML are initially considered members. Members may not participate in official votes, nor will the have access to Executive Board positions. They may contribute to MML development and research projects under the guidance and supervision of an Executive Board member or a project lead.

Developers

Developers are individuals invested in the MML community. These members have full voting rights and may request project leadership positions. A project leadership position entails access to MML infrastructure as required by their project, and as overseen by the VP of Projects. Additionally, project leadership entails the responsibility to maintain project documentation and oversee project development. Developers may also contribute to MML projects under the guidance and supervision of a project lead. To attain developer status, a member must demonstrate leadership skills, commitment to MML principles, and technical ability. Prospective developers will be selected based on these qualifications by official vote.

Any prospective developer will not be voted on more than twice per semester. Any developer who leaves the university, whether by graduating or otherwise, loses their developer status. Developers who graduate automatically become alumni members. Developers who leave the university without graduating do not automatically gain alumni status, but may request alumni status from the Executive Board, who will decide the matter by a majority vote. Developers who are expelled lose their developer status and may not request alumni status.

Becoming a Developer

To attain developer status, a member must demonstrate leadership skills, commitment to MML principles, and technical ability. To become a developer, a member (hereafter referred to as a “nominee”) should notify the Executive Board of their desire to become a developer and present a justification for gaining developer status. This justification should include a brief history of the nominee’s contributions to the organization. Alternatively, a developer may nominate a member to the Executive Board and provide a similar rationale.

The Executive Board will review the rationale and vote on whether to admit the nominee. If the Executive Board votes against admitting the developer, then the developer body (including the members of the Executive Board) shall be provided with the nominee’s rationale and will have their own chance to vote, conducted according to the rules of an official vote. If the developers vote to admit the nominee, they shall be admitted without prejudice. If the developers vote to not admit the nominee, then the nominee shall not be given developer status, but may re-apply in the future.

No nominee may be voted on more than twice in one semester.

Developer Selection Criteria

To be accepted as a developer, an individual must meet three of the following four criteria, with emphasis on the first two.

  • MATH 203, CS 310; B or higher.
  • Active contributions to MML development projects.
  • Active leadership participation in MML community events.
  • Active social participation in the MML community, including meeting or event attendance.

Course requirements may be waved if a sufficient level of competency in linear algebra, statistics, and algorithms is demonstrated.

Researchers

Researchers are individuals invested in the MML community. These members have full voting rights and may request research leadership positions. A research leadership position entails access to MML infrastructure as required by their research, and as overseen by the VP of Research. Additionally, research leadership entails the responsibility to maintain research documentation and oversee research development. Researchers may also contribute to MML research under the guidance and supervision of a research lead. To attain researcher status, a member must demonstrate leadership skills, commitment to MML principles, and technical ability. Prospective researchers will be selected based on these qualifications by official vote.

Any prospective researcher will not be voted on more than twice per semester. Any researcher who leaves the university, whether by graduating or otherwise, loses their researcher status. Researchers who graduate automatically become alumni members. Developers who leave the university without graduating do not automatically gain alumni status, but may request alumni status from the Executive Board, who will decide the matter by a majority vote. Researchers who are expelled lose their researcher status and may not request alumni status.

Becoming a Researcher

To attain researcher status, a member must demonstrate leadership skills, commitment to MML principles, and technical ability. To become a researcher, a member (hereafter referred to as a “nominee”) should notify the Executive Board of their desire to become a researcher and present a justification for gaining researcher status. This justification should include a brief history of the nominee’s contributions to the organization. Alternatively, a researcher may nominate a member to the Executive Board and provide a similar rationale.

The Executive Board will review the rationale and vote on whether to admit the nominee. If the Executive Board votes against admitting the researcher, then the researcher body (including the members of the Executive Board) shall be provided with the nominee’s rationale and will have their own chance to vote, conducted according to the rules of an official vote. If the researchers vote to admit the nominee, they shall be admitted without prejudice. If the researchers vote to not admit the nominee, then the nominee shall not be given researcher status, but may re-apply in the future.

No nominee may be voted on more than twice in one semester.

Research Selection Criteria

To be accepted as a researcher, an individual must meet three of the following four criteria, with emphasis on the first two.

  • MATH 203, MATH 213, STAT 250/344; B or higher
  • Active contributions to MML research projects
  • Active leadership participation in MML community events.
  • Active social participation in the MML community, including meeting or event attendance.

Course requirements may be waved if a sufficient level of competency in linear algebra, statistics, and multivariable calculus is demonstrated.

Revocation of Membership

In extraordinary circumstances, if a motion is brought and seconded at a meeting for revocation of a esteemed member's status, the MML general membership will be notified by email of the motion not less than five days before the following meeting. If a quorum of esteemed members is present, an official vote will be held by secret ballot, requiring a $\frac{3}{4}$ of the esteemed membership. Although an official vote, all present members will be allowed to present arguments for or against revocation of the esteemed member's status prior to the vote.

Developer status will be automatically removed if suspended or expelled by the university.

Article IV - Officers

The term for a given officer lasts until the end of the spring semester; there are no term limits. Non-President members may hold at most two office positions. The Executive Board is a set of six individuals whom uphold the constitution and maintain the structure of MML. All officers will attend any training required by OSI.

President

The President of MML presides over all meetings; serves as spokesperson for MML; acts as its main liaison to OSI; oversees the transition to next semester’s officers, and ensures MML fulfills its constitutional obligations. The President will also deliver lectures pertaining to Machine Learning during club meetings.

Vice-President of Finance (Treasurer)

The VP of Finance keeps accurate records of any expenditures and accounting as outlined by the Office of Student Involvement’s “Fiscal Management Policies and Forms.”

Vice-President of Research

The VP of Research acts as an intellectual advisor for MML, oversees the development of novel ideas, organizes the collaboration of members who hold the title 'Researcher', delivers lectures during club meetings pertaining to Machine Learning, and strives toward the development of research in Machine Learning.

Vice-President of Projects

The VP of Projects oversees and approves the projects being developed by MML, manages the version control systems used for each project, teaches members with the title of 'Developer' about Machine Learning concepts/implementations, and delivers lectures pertaining to Machine Learning during club meetings.

Vice-President of Internal Affairs (Secretary)

The VP of Internal Affairs takes minutes at each meeting and publishes them to MML’s website, keeps record of the membership status of MML members, coordinates advertisement campaigns with the VP of External Affairs, and maintains all other necessary records and files.

Vice-President of External Affairs

The VP of External Affairs maintains and evolves the MML website, represents the organization through social media outlets, coordinates advertisement campaigns with the VP of Internal Affairs, and maintains all other necessary records and files.

Article V - Elections
Standard Elections

Elections will be held during the first meetings of March and November, with the results to come into effect at the beginning of the following semester.

Voting shall be handled in a secret ballot to be counted by a trusted member who was unanimously chosen by the board who is also not in contest for any officer position, or if all positions are in contention, then by a quorum of all project leads not directly involved in elections.

In the event of a tie, a second round of voting will be held between the top two candidates following the same procedures of the first round.

In the event of a further tie, the two candidates will participate in a 15-minute coding competition.

Special Elections

In event of the resignation of an officer, removal of an officer, or any other situation in which there is a vacant executive position, an election will be held to fill that position for the remainder of the term following the procedures above.

Article VI - Impeachment or Resignation

While an officer is in an elected position, they must fulfill the responsibilities of that position. If an elected officer fails to perform their responsibilities or abuses the privileges of their position, they will be subjected to impeachment and a subsequent removal of office.

Removal of Officers

Two MML esteemed members may present a motion to remove an officer. The general membership will be notified by email, and the esteemed membership will have no fewer than five days to review the motion. If a quorum is present at the next MML meeting, the 3⁄4 motion may be approved by a vote by secret ballot of the present esteemed membership. This is considered an official vote.

Officer Resignation

In the event that an elected officer no longer wishes to hold their position, they shall announce their decision at the next MML Organizational meeting. If they are not able to attend the meeting, then they are responsible for finding another method to communicate this decision to the other officers.

Vacant Positions

If an elected position becomes vacant due to an impeachment or resignation, a special election will be held to fill that vacant position as described in Article V.II.

Article VII - Meetings

MML shall have two official meetings each week. Meeting times will be scheduled to accommodate the largest amount of members. All members of the Mason community are welcome to attend meetings. MML members, developers, and researchers are encouraged to attend these meetings, but attendance is not strictly required; however, consistent absenteeism is considered grounds for the revocation of one's membership status.

The Executive Board has the right to call its own private meetings at its discretion.

A quorum shall be defined to include at least two officers, and either a simple majority of esteemed members or seven total esteemed members including at least two officers, whichever is less. On official votes, in the event of a tie, the deciding vote shall be cast by the presiding officer.

The President shall preside over all meetings. In the event of the President’s absence, the meeting shall be presided over by the highest ranking available officer.

The most recent edition of Robert’s Rules of Order will guide meeting procedure.

Meetings will consist of at least one of two possible discrete sections, an organizational section and a development section. What type of meeting is scheduled for a particular week will be made clear at the time of the meeting’s announcement.

Organizational Meeting

Organizational meetings are to present motions, discuss status of MML initiatives and other matters of interest or concern to MML, vote on germane resolutions, and other matters permitted by MML’s officers. All members of the Mason community are welcome to present topics of discussion at meetings. The general membership may vote on non-official resolutions.

Research & Development Meeting

R&D meetings are not subject to the rigorous standards outlined above. They are entirely optional meetings. R&D meetings will be used to provide the general membership with an opportunity to meet in person and work together on projects in a relaxed atmosphere. There are no time or attendance limits, requirements, or expectations placed on development meetings.

Article VIII.I - Finance

No dues shall ever be required as part of MML membership.

Article VIII.II - Amendments

Two esteemed members may jointly propose an explicitly defined constitutional amendment at a meeting. The general membership will be emailed a copy of the language of the amendment and have no fewer than five days to review the proposed amendment. At the next MML meeting, if a quorum is present, the amendment may be approved by a 3⁄4 vote by secret ballot of the present esteemed membership. This is considered an official vote.

The Office of Student Involvement must review all amendments in the same matter as a completely new constitution.

Changes which do not affect the meaning of the text, such as updated formatting and links, may be made at the discretion of the board.

While members may not directly propose amendments, they may convince an esteemed member to sponsor an amendment on their behalf. Developers and researchers should only sponsor an amendment if they agree with the changes to be made.

Article IX - Ratification

This constitution shall become effective upon approval by a 3⁄4 vote of the esteemed membership, and a Student Involvement staff member. This is considered an official vote.
